Visual Tips

You don't need to know design — just follow these 6 simple rules.

01
Use Breathing Room
Don't pack everything together
Leave space between sections, text, and images. A spacious layout feels more professional and is easier to read. When in doubt — add more space.
02
Stick to 2 Fonts
One for headings, one for text
Using too many fonts makes your site look messy. Pick one bold font for titles and one clean font for normal text. Most website builders have built-in font pairs — just pick one you like.
03
Use Your Brand Colors
2–3 colors maximum
Your main color (matches your logo), a light background (white or off-white), and one highlight color for buttons. More than 3 colors starts to look unprofessional.
04
Make Text Easy to Read
Dark text on a light background
Dark grey or black text on a white background. If you have to squint to read it, your customers won't bother. Never put light text on a light background.
05
Check It on Your Phone
Most visitors are on mobile
Over 70% of your visitors will view your site on a phone. After building, always preview it on a phone screen before publishing. Most builders do this automatically.
06
One Clear Action Per Page
Don't give visitors too many choices
Every page should push visitors toward one main action — calling you, filling a form, or booking. The more options you give, the less likely they are to do anything.
