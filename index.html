Design Principles

6 rules that separate good websites from great ones.

01
8px Grid System
8 · 16 · 24 · 32 · 48 · 64px
Every margin, padding, and gap should be a multiple of 8. This creates invisible visual harmony that users feel even if they can't explain it.
02
Max 2 Fonts
e.g. Syne (heading) + DM Sans (body)
One distinctive display font for headings, one clean font for body text. Every extra font adds visual noise and slows load time.
03
60-30-10 Color Rule
Neutral 60% · Base 30% · Accent 10%
Your accent color is your brand fingerprint. Use it sparingly — for CTAs, highlights, and key interactions only. Less = more impact.
04
4.5:1 Contrast Ratio
WCAG AA minimum — check webaim.org
Text must have at least 4.5:1 contrast against its background. Accessibility isn't optional — it's good design and good SEO.
05
Mobile-First Always
375px → 768px → 1280px
Over 60% of web traffic is mobile. Start your layout at 375px. If it looks great on mobile, it will look great everywhere.
06
Whitespace = Confidence
When in doubt — add more space
Generous whitespace signals quality. It's the same reason luxury brands use it. Resist the urge to fill every pixel.
